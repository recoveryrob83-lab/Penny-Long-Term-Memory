## Purpose

Capture a real-world service-business lead leak observed by Rob while helping coordinate a house cleanout project.

This is not a client record and should remain abstract. Do not store company names, personal names, addresses, phone numbers, prices, or private project details in GitHub.

## Observation

A local service business was contacted as a potential provider for a cleanout / large-item removal project.

The business gave one of the strongest initial responses among the options considered. They provided useful information and gave a rough estimated price without requiring an in-person visit first.

That initial response created trust and positioned them as a serious contender.

However, after that first contact, there was no apparent follow-up.

No follow-up call.
No follow-up text.
No request for photos.
No attempt to schedule a formal estimate.
No clear next step.

The potential customer was warm and interested, but the business allowed the opportunity to stall.

## Why This Matters

This is a clean example of a Lead Leak.

The company did not necessarily have a marketing problem. They generated or received the lead.

They did not necessarily have a trust problem. Their initial response was helpful.

The leak occurred after first contact, in the transition from interested lead to active customer.

The missing system was not more advertising. The missing system was reliable follow-up and next-action control.

## Lead-to-Client Failure Pattern

Observed path:

1. Customer need exists.
2. Customer contacts service business.
3. Business responds with useful information.
4. Customer becomes a warm potential buyer.
5. No next action is captured or assigned.
6. No follow-up occurs.
7. Potential job remains unconverted.

## Business Insight

A Lead Leak is not only a missed call or lost web form.

A Lead Leak can happen after a strong first conversation when nobody owns the next step.

The core problem is often:

> The business has interest, but no reliable path from interest to approval.

## Possible Service Framing

Office Leaks Consulting can position the Lead Leak Sprint around this problem:

> You may not need more leads. You may need a better way to stop warm leads from disappearing after the first conversation.

Or:

> We help service businesses capture the next step so interested customers do not fall through the cracks.

## Lead-to-Client Workflow Draft

For Lead Leak work, the relevant workflow may be:

Lead → Response → Qualification → Estimate / Appointment → Follow-Up → Approval → Active Job

This may be more useful for the first service offer than the full lead-to-payment workflow.
