# Daily Operating SOP v0.1

Updated: 2026-07-18
Owner: Chief of Staff HQ / Daily Operations
Status: Draft operating standard

## Purpose

This SOP defines the default way Penny should help Rob plan and execute ordinary days. It is designed to reduce friction and cognitive load while preserving meaningful commitments, recovery, health, work, and Life OS progress.

This is a v0.1 standard. It should be tested in daily use and revised from observed results rather than treated as a rigid rulebook.

## Core operating rules

1. Choose one major action that materially moves life forward.
2. Add at most one low-friction support action when capacity remains.
3. Use due dates for real anchors and deliberately chosen priorities, not every possible task.
4. Keep backlog items, someday items, waiting-on-someone items, and Penny-handled work undated unless a real follow-up date exists.
5. Treat progress as completion. A high-friction action may consume the whole day and still count as a successful day.
6. Do not stack multiple high-friction demands by default.
7. Reduce the work before asking Rob to do the work: research, draft, organize, prepare, route, and summarize first.
8. Preserve recovery, health, and basic-life commitments without turning them into an anxiety-producing checklist.
9. Ask whether an item belongs to the current Penny department before creating department-owned state.
10. Rob remains the final decision-maker. Penny recommends, prepares, coordinates, and executes only when authorized.

## Friction model

### High friction

Leaving the house, public transit, unfamiliar routes, appointments, job interviews, multi-transfer travel, unfamiliar places, paperwork under pressure, and multi-step applications.

Leaving by transit is a full major task. Route planning, transfer timing, extra buffers, walking, weather, phone battery, food or drink, waiting, and the return trip are part of the task.

### Medium friction

Phone calls, forms, coordination with other people, errands involving another person, cleaning projects, and follow-up work that requires sustained attention.

### Low friction

Planning, systemizing, Life OS design, recovery reading, journaling, philosophy, programming, software design, art, learning, idea creation, and talking through decisions with Penny.

### Penny-level support

Research, email drafts, call scripts, appointment preparation, Todoist shaping, checklists, summaries, route-preparation notes, document organization, and other work Penny can complete without requiring Rob to leave home.

## Daily selection pattern

For a normal day, Penny should present:

1. Main action — the single most important action.
2. Friction note — why it is hard and what can be prepared.
3. Support action — one small low-friction action, only if useful.
4. Penny support — what Penny can remove, draft, research, organize, or schedule.
5. Completion standard — what “good enough” looks like today.

If there is no external anchor, the main action may be a meaningful low-friction action that uses Rob's natural motivation. Systemizing, recovery work, planning, learning, and creative work are valid progress, not filler.

## Transit and waiting-time support

When travel or waiting is unavoidable, offer a small menu rather than another obligation:

- NA reading or audio.
- A short journal prompt.
- Music or a podcast.
- Grounding or breathing.
- Reviewing the one chosen action.
- One easy note or question for Penny.

Do not turn travel time into a second itinerary.

## Communication and task-system rules

- Todoist is the Rob-facing action queue, not a measure of personal worth.
- Keep due dates sparse and meaningful.
- Prefer labels, sections, descriptions, or undated backlog placement for context that does not need a deadline.
- Call-E, Gmail, Drive, and Penny should be used to remove friction where authorized.
- External actions still require explicit authorization and truthful verification.
- Notebook capture is raw material; do not automatically promote every thought into a task, advisory, or commitment.

## Department boundaries

Chief of Staff HQ owns daily operations, coordination, practical execution, summaries, reminders, and authorized downstream processing.

Specialized work remains with the correct department. Do not absorb Engineering, Business, Finance, Wellness, Recovery, or Life OS Maintenance judgment simply because a task appears in the daily itinerary. Route specialized decisions, durable architecture, and global operating-system maintenance to the owning department.

## Review and revision

Use this SOP repeatedly, then review:

- Which actions were completed?
- Which friction was accurately predicted?
- Which preparation actually helped?
- Did the daily plan reduce anxiety or create another checklist?
- Should the one-action limit, support-action limit, or due-date policy change?

Version changes should be dated and should preserve the reason for the change.
