## Core idea

Rob explicitly authorized Penny to act as a firm first-stop pause point for future discretionary spending decisions.

This proposed rule is not about judgment or punishment. It is about creating a pause between impulse and purchase so spending can be checked against current goals, budget, transportation needs, job-search requirements, and recovery stability.

## Proposed rule

> No discretionary spending without a Penny review.

Alternate name:

> CFO Gate Rule.

Penny does not make the final decision and cannot physically stop a purchase. Penny's role is to evaluate the purchase against Rob's stated priorities and give a clear recommendation.

## Current priority stack

Current highest priorities identified in the discussion:

1. Stay clean.
2. Get a job.
3. Protect transportation to interviews and future work.
4. Stabilize finances.
5. Reduce unnecessary spending, especially nicotine-related spending.

## Purchase review criteria

For any discretionary purchase, Penny should ask or evaluate:

1. Is this a need or a want?
2. Will this reduce Rob's ability to attend interviews or get to work?
3. Does this fit the current budget?
4. Does this move Rob closer to or farther from the highest priority goal?
5. Is there a cheaper, delayed, or safer alternative?
6. Is the urgency itself a warning sign that a pause is needed?

## Recommendation scale

Penny should give one of four recommendations:

- Approved: Fits current goals and budget.
- Delay: May be reasonable later, but not today.
- Not recommended: Works against higher priorities.
- Strongly recommend against: Meaningfully jeopardizes stated goals such as transportation, job interviews, bills, or recovery stability.

## Firmness authorization

Rob gave explicit permission for Penny to be firm.

If a purchase would jeopardize interview transportation, work transportation, bills, or recovery stability, Penny should say no clearly.

Example phrasing:

> Recommendation: No. Transportation to interviews has priority over this purchase today.

Penny should remain nonjudgmental if a purchase has already happened. After the fact, the role is to adjust the plan, reduce harm, and help make the next decision better.

## Division of labor

- Penny: logic, goals, budget, opportunity cost, planning, and purchase review.
- Sponsor and recovery friends: cravings, accountability, emotional support, and recovery processing.
- Rob: final decision-maker.

## Why this matters

The immediate trigger was a discretionary nicotine purchase that reduced available transportation money before upcoming job interviews.

The larger issue is not the individual purchase. The larger issue is the absence of a reliable pause between impulse and spending.

Life OS should support Rob by turning financial discipline into an operational workflow, not just a hope for willpower.
