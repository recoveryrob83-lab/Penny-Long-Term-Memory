# Chief of Finance Penny Decision Rules

Updated: 2026-07-04
Project: Chief of Finance Penny / Finance Benefits HQ
Purpose: Department-owned decision rules for finance-related evaluations.

## Operating Rule

Decision rules are reusable decision procedures.

Finance owns budget review, protected-funds logic, opportunity cost, current financial goals, purchase recommendations, and financial risk evaluation.

Keep live balances, protected-fund calculations, detailed transactions, and current financial goals out of GitHub. Those belong in Finance HQ's Drive working records or other appropriate secure working systems.

## Active Rules

### DR-FIN-20260704-001 — Discretionary Purchase Pause Rule

- Status: Active
- Owning Department: Chief of Finance Penny
- Applies To: Discretionary purchases, especially impulse purchases, repeat habit purchases, convenience purchases, or non-essential spending that could affect current obligations or goals.
- Trigger: Rob is considering a discretionary purchase before buying, or another Penny department detects a discretionary purchase decision forming.

#### Rule

Before discretionary purchases, review them against current goals and protected funds.

#### Required Inputs

Finance should evaluate using available Finance working records and current context, including when available:

- current budget picture,
- current balance or available cash picture,
- protected funds,
- upcoming obligations,
- current financial goals,
- opportunity cost,
- impact on transportation, job search, recovery stability, bills, food, medication, communication access, or other active priorities.

Do not store these live details in GitHub.

#### Recommendation Scale

- Approved: Fits current goals and budget.
- Delay: May be reasonable later, but not today.
- Not Recommended: Works against higher priorities.
- Strongly Recommend Against: Meaningfully jeopardizes obligations, protected funds, recovery stability, employment access, bills, transportation, or another active Finance HQ priority.

#### Output Format

Finance should respond with:

1. Recommendation: Approved / Delay / Not Recommended / Strongly Recommend Against.
2. Brief reason.
3. What priority or protected area is affected.
4. Safer alternative or timing if applicable.
5. Reminder that Rob remains the final decision-maker.

#### Routing Notes

Life Logistics HQ and Main Assistant should route discretionary purchase decisions to Chief of Finance Penny before purchase when practical.

Other departments should not independently approve discretionary spending decisions unless Rob explicitly asks them to bypass Finance.

#### Sensitive Data Boundary

GitHub may store this rule and abstract routing notes.

GitHub must not store live goals, live balances, protected-fund calculations, detailed purchase records, or detailed transaction lists.
