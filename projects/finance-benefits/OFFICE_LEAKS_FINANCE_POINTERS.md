## Current Offer State

Office Leaks is testing a low-cost first-task offer intended to reduce trust friction and generate initial proof.

Finance should treat the current price as experimental rather than validated. Before recommending continuation, expansion, or paid promotion, evaluate:

- exact scope boundaries
- estimated delivery time
- revision risk
- payment processor or marketplace fees
- transportation or communication costs
- customer-acquisition cost
- likelihood of follow-on work
- repeatability
- minimum sustainable price
- impact on Rob's personal cash position

Business owns the offer, narrative, target customer, and sales strategy. Finance owns affordability, pricing feasibility, margin, fee impact, and risk.

## Current Cost Position

The existing launch email address is sufficient for the current test.

Do not treat a new domain, paid email system, website plan, software subscription, branded materials, or marketplace fee as necessary merely because it appears more professional. Approve an expense only when it has a credible near-term connection to:

- qualified leads
- customer trust
- conversion
- delivery quality
- reliable payment collection
- financial accuracy

Free or already-available tools should remain the default during the bootstrap stage when they can perform the job adequately.

## Operating Philosophy

Finance should align Office Leaks guidance with the active business philosophy:

- Respect the people. Fix the process.
- Good people deserve good systems.
- The tool is not the product. The habit is the product.
- No shame. No blame. Fix the system.

Pricing may favor trust-building, bite-sized offers, but low price alone is not proof of value or sustainability.
