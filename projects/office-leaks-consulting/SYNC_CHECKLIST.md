Do not load another department's broad backlog merely for visibility.

## Sync Questions

1. Has the live Office Leaks execution state changed?
2. Have Office Leaks-owned offers, packages, outreach, delivery promises, market tests, sales activity, or customer-facing operations changed?
3. Has real audience or inquiry evidence created a parent-level market, positioning, offer-strategy, commercial-priority, portfolio, or business-model question for Business HQ?
4. Has Engineering HQ updated a technical implementation, delivery architecture, software, automation, template, or tooling dependency that Office Leaks must consume?
5. Has Finance HQ updated a cost-bearing, affordability, pricing-feasibility, startup-cost, revenue, paid-promotion, or tax dependency that Office Leaks must consume?
6. Has Chief of Staff HQ routed a concrete assignment, report request, scheduling need, reminder, or practical support item that affects Office Leaks execution?
7. Has Life OS Maintenance HQ routed a specific governance, boot, source-boundary, audit, or repository-reconciliation dependency?
8. Are archived `virtual-assistant-business` records being used as current Office Leaks authority rather than historical evidence?
9. Is Office Leaks work duplicated in Business, System, Chief of Staff, Maintenance, Engineering, Finance, or another department merely for visibility?
10. Is LifeOS HQ being described as a department, independent authority, or backlog owner?
11. Are any open advisories actually addressed to Office Leaks HQ or does another department need to act, decide, monitor, or accept responsibility?

## Update Rules

Update `SESSION_HANDOFF.md` after meaningful project-state changes.

Update `status.md` when phase, ownership, or current priority changes.

Update `open_loops.md` when Office Leaks-owned loops are created, closed, clarified, or reprioritized.

Use the notebook only for promoted reasoning, evidence, decisions, experiments, validation, discoveries, operating lessons, or meaningful history with durable value. Raw ideas and uncommitted possibilities normally remain in Trello.

Update Captain's Log only for meaningful cross-project architecture changes or major operational lessons owned by the appropriate system role.

No meaningful change means no write.

## Guardrails

- Keep GitHub abstract and non-sensitive.
- Do not store client private details, live financial records, tax records, payment records, contracts, credentials, or identifying operational details.
- Do not silently absorb Business HQ parent-strategy decisions or duplicate its strategy records.
- Do not copy Office Leaks execution work into another department or system file merely for visibility.
- Do not make cost-bearing or finance decisions; route them to Finance HQ.
- Do not make technical implementation or architecture decisions; route them to Engineering HQ.
- Do not turn daily reminders into Office Leaks durable state; route practical coordination to Chief of Staff HQ.
- Do not route routine Office Leaks durable state to Life OS Maintenance HQ.
- Do not treat LifeOS HQ discussion as authority or durable state.
- Do not create an advisory unless a real unresolved cross-department dependency remains.
