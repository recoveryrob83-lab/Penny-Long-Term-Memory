# Recovery Logistics

Status: Active
Updated: 2026-07-02
Registry Ref: REF-006

## Purpose

Keep recovery anchors visible and connected to practical life planning.

This project tracks recovery logistics, routines, milestones, and operational next actions. It is not a private inventory file, sponsor transcript, or deep spiritual/philosophy archive.

## Current Focus

- Maintain daily recovery routine.
- Maintain weekly contact commitments.
- Respect meeting schedule when planning work, appointments, or job logistics.
- Use recovery structure as a stabilizer, not a perfection trap.
- Keep recovery actions visible in Todoist and timed commitments visible in Calendar.

## Project Files

- `README.md` — project purpose, boundaries, routing.
- `status.md` — current recovery logistics state.
- `routines.md` — daily and weekly anchor structure.
- `milestones.md` — dated recovery milestones and meaningful progress markers.
- `open_loops.md` — unfinished recovery logistics actions.

## Tool Routing

- Todoist: daily and weekly anchors.
- Calendar: scheduled meetings, calls, interviews, appointments, and recovery-related time blocks.
- Drive: working recovery documents, step notes, PDFs, journals, and long-form reflections.
- GitHub: durable recovery logistics state and abstract references.
- Gmail: recovery-related communications when relevant.
- SMS/phone messages: sponsor/friend logistics or meeting details shared by Rob.
- Weather: transportation/weather planning when needed.

## Boundaries

Keep this project practical.

Do not store:
- Private inventory details.
- Sponsor/private fellowship details beyond operational usefulness.
- Sensitive medical or identifying information.
- Full personal stories that belong in Drive, chat logs, or recovery writing.

Do store:
- Current step/logistics state.
- Routine structure.
- Milestones.
- Next actions.
- Source-system routing.
- Lessons about what helps Rob stay organized in recovery.

## Operating Principle

Recovery logistics supports recovery; it does not replace meetings, sponsor work, fellowship, honesty, or lived action.
