# Recovery Routines

Updated: 2026-07-02

## Purpose

Document the recovery routine structure that Todoist and Calendar help support.

This file describes the pattern, not the private content of the work.

## Daily Anchors

Current daily anchor set:

- Daily Step Work.
- Daily It Works Reading.
- Daily Meditation Reading.
- Daily Story Reading.
- Daily Call Donald.
- Daily Recovery Journal.
- Daily Step 11 Prayer.

## Weekly Anchors

Current weekly anchor set:

- Call three recovery people.
- Maintain meeting attendance rhythm.
- Check whether recovery commitments need to be protected when scheduling work, interviews, appointments, or errands.

## Scheduling Guidance

Use Todoist for recurring anchors.
Use Calendar only when an anchor has a specific time, such as a meeting, scheduled call, appointment, or planned time block.

When Rob asks for an itinerary, include recovery anchors as flexible blocks unless exact times are known.

## Smallest-Viable Routine Rule

If the day becomes crowded, preserve the smallest workable version of the routine.

Possible scaled-down form:

- One reading.
- One prayer or meditation moment.
- One recovery contact or meeting action.
- One short journal note.

This is a practical planning rule, not sponsor advice.

## Review Rule

If recurring Todoist anchors pile up as overdue, help Rob sort, reschedule, or complete them without turning the routine into a shame machine.
